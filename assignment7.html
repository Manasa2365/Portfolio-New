Design of 6-Degree of Freedom Robotic Arm in Fusion 360

Introduction:

This project focuses on designing a 6-degree of freedom (6-DOF) robotic arm using Fusion 360 as part of the Industrial Automation session. The model mimics the motion of industrial robot arms used in manufacturing, packaging, welding, and precision tasks.

Project Highlights:

  • Realistic joint-based motion: base, shoulder, elbow, wrist, gripper
  • Parametric design with accurate degrees of freedom
  • Modular and scalable CAD architecture
  • Assembly with precise constraints and axis alignment

Design Workflow:

  1. Base Design: Started with a stable rotating base with mounting holes
  2. Shoulder & Elbow: Created as hollow sections for motor & gear mounting
  3. Wrist & End Effector: Designed gripper with parallel jaw structure
  4. Assembly: Used Joint, Revolute, and As-Built Joint tools for motion planning

Conclusion:

This 6-DOF robotic arm model demonstrates my understanding of mechanical design for motion systems, CAD assembly planning, and industrial applications. In future, I aim to simulate its motion using Fusion 360 animation workspace or integrate it with physical servo motors and microcontrollers.
